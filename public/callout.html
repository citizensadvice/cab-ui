Callouts

Used as part of content formats to highlight information. This can be a few words, paragraph or paragraphs with multiple links.

It is important to ask:

  • is the callout neccessary, too many, misused or unneccessary callouts weaken their value
  • which style of callout is relevant, is the information 'useful', 'important' or 'recommending'

Colours can't be relied upon as the only method of communicating the importance and content of a callout. Any callouts should be clearly titled using h2 or h3 tags. Use the appropriate Aria tags for the relevant callouts to ensure screenreaders understand the information contained.

Callout styles

Callout unstyled

This callout does not require an Aria tag but should be labeled with a clear heading.

This style is used for any neutral callouts, where the information needs to be seperated from the content. It should also be used for examples.

<div class="callout">
	<h3>Callout unstyled</h3>
	<p>This callout does not require an Aria tag but should be labeled with a clear heading.</p>
	<p>This style is used for any neutral callouts, where the information needs to be seperated from the content. It should also be used for examples.</p>
</div>

Callout warning

Use the Aria tag aria-label="Important".

The warning callout should be used for any important snippet of text that has serious and/or legal implications if the client does not follow the advice.

<div class="callout--warning">
	<h3>Callout warning</h3>
	<p>Use the Aria tag <code>aria-label="Important"</code>.</p>
	<p>The warning callout should be used for any important snippet of text that has serious and/or legal implications if the client does not follow the advice.</p>
</div>

Callout alert

Use the Aria tag aria-label="Help".

This callout style should be applied on information which is important and will have implications on the client if they do understand and follow the advice but it isn't mandatory and won't have legal implications they don't follow.

Callout alert with icon

The icon is optional and can be including by adding the .callout--icon class.de aria-label="Help"].

<div class="callout--alert callout--icon">
	<h3>Callout alert</h3>
	<p>Use the Aria tag <code>aria-label="Help"</code>.</p>
	<p>This callout style should be applied on information which is important and will have implications on the client if they do understand and follow the advice but it isn't mandatory and won't have legal implications they don't follow.</p>
</div>
<div class="callout--alert callout--icon">
	<h3>Callout alert with icon</h3>
	<p>The icon is optional and can be including by adding the <code>.callout--icon</code> class.</p>
</div>

Callout confirmation

This callout is used to confirm an action as correct or where the content needs to act as positive reinforcement of the informaiton i.e. 'Do apply for this if…'

Callout confirmation with icon

The icon is optional and can be including by adding the .callout--icon class.

<div class="callout--confirmation">
	<h3>Callout confirmation</h3>
	<p>This callout is used to confirm an action as correct or where the content needs to act as positive reinforcement of the informaiton i.e. 'Do apply for this if…'</p>
</div>
<div class="callout--confirmation callout--icon">
	<h3>Callout confirmation with icon</h3>
	<p>The icon is optional and can be including by adding the <code>.callout--icon</code> class.</p>
</div>

Callout quote

Also referred to as Blockquote, this style is when a quote needs to be displayed.

<div class="callout--quote">
	<h3>Callout quote</h3>
	<p>Also referred to as Blockquote, this style is when a quote needs to be displayed.</p>
</div>

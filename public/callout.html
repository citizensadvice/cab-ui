Citizens Advice elements guide

Used as part of content formats to highlight information. This can be a few words, paragraph or paragraphs with multiple links.

It is important to ask:

  • is the callout neccessary, too many, misused or unneccessary callouts weaken their value
  • which style of callout is relevant, is the information 'useful', 'important' or 'recommending'

Colours can't be relied upon as the only method of communicating the importance and content of a callout. Any callouts should be clearly titled using h2orh3 tags. Use the appropriate Aria tags for the relevant callouts to ensure screenreaders understand the information contained.

Callout styles

Callout unstyled

div.callout

This callout does not require an Aria tag but should be labeled with a clear heading.

This style is used for any neutral callouts, where the information needs to be seperated from the content. It should also be used for examples.

a.callout__link

Callout confirmation

div.callout--confirmation

This callout is used to confirm an action as correct or where the content needs to act as positive reinforcement of the informaiton i.e. 'Do apply for this if…'

a.callout__link

Callout warning

div.callout--warning.callout--icon

Use the Aria tag aria-label="Important".

The warning callout should be used for any important snippet of text that has serious and/or legal implications if the client does not follow the advice.

a.callout__link

Callout alert

div.callout--alert.callout--icon

Use the Aria tag aria-label="Help".

This callout style should be applied on information which is important and will have implications on the client if they do understand and follow the advice but it isn't mandatory and won't have legal implications they don't follow.

a.callout__link

Callout quote

div.callout--quote

Also referred to as Blockquote, this style is when a quote needs to be displayed.

a.callout__link

Family members joining you in the UK to live

This advice is for EmailPrint

This is level 1 advice - you don't need to be OISC-accredited to advise on the immigration issues covered on this page. Find out more about what you can and can’t advise on.

This is level 2 advice - you can’t advise on the immigration issues covered on this page unless you have a level 2 or 3 OISC qualification.

If you’re not qualified, you can:

  • still talk through what’s on this page (so long as you’re comfortable doing so)
  • refer the client to an adviser or immigration specialist accredited at OISC level 2 or higher - search for an immigration adviser on GOV.UK if you don’t have someone reputable in your area

If you’re already in the UK, your family members (eg husband, wife, child) will need visas to join you to live for over 6 months. If they’re coming for a visit for less than 6 months you need to apply for a visit visa.

If successful, they’ll normally be able to stay in the uk for 2 or 3 years. After that, they’ll usually be able to apply to extend the visa or settle in the UK - also known as getting ‘indefinite leave to remain’.

A family member who joins you in the UK on the basis of your right to stay in the UK is called a ‘dependant’. You can apply for them.

If the application gets rejected, an appeal will take 6 to 9 months, so you should think about getting help from a specialist immigration adviser. You can:

  • search for an immigration adviser on GOV.UK
  • contact your local Citizens Advice for help finding someone in your area

Check what visa they need

The visa your family member needs will depend on your circumstances.

Your circumstances The visa they need
You’re a British citizen, or naturalised ‘Family of a settled person’ visa
You’re a ‘settled person’ in the UK (ie you have ‘indefinite leave to remain’) ‘Family of a settled person’ visa
You’re an EEA national living in the UK and your dependant is not from the EEA ‘EEA family permit’
You have refugee status or humanitarian protection Family reunion
You have a Tier 2 (General) visa Apply as a dependant of a Tier 2 (General) visa holder
You have a Tier 4 student visa Apply as a dependant of a Tier 4 visa holder

If you’re applying for them

You can fill in the application form for your family member - you need to do this online through the links above.

After you do the form, your family member will need to have their fingerprints and photograph taken at a visa application centre. Check where their nearest visa application centre is before you apply, because it might be in a different country.

The online application system doesn’t list the visas by name - you’ll have to answer some questions to find the visa you need. There’s an option to “apply for someone else” on the online form.

You need to make sure you put the your family member’s information as the applicant’s details.

Include the correct evidence

The most common reason a visa application gets rejected is because there’s not enough evidence (ie documents that prove your case) sent with the application.

In general, you’ll need to provide a piece of evidence to support everything you say in the application. Make sure you carefully follow the online guidance for the visa you’re applying for.

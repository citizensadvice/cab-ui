    Check if you're eligible for Universal Credit

    Universal Credit is a new benefit that you can only get in some parts of the UK.

    If youre living in a Universal Credit area, you might need to apply for Universal Credit instead of other existing benefits like Jobseeker's Allowance, Housing Benefit or Tax Credits etc.

    Its mainly available to people who are unemployed or earn less than 338 a month - but you can get it in some other circumstances.

    Get help

    It can be complicated to work out if youre eligible just by reading all the small print - the rules are outlined in full below.

    It might be quicker and easier to talk to an expert so you can explain your personal circumstances. You can do this in a few ways:

    • call the Universal Credit helpline on 0345 600 0723 (Textphone: 0345 600 0743). Calls can cost 9p a minute from a landline, or between 8p and 40p a minute from a mobile (your phone supplier can tell you how much youll pay). You can call and ask them to call you back
    • contact your local Citizens Advice

    Who can apply for Universal Credit

    You can currently only apply in a Universal Credit area

    The first thing you should do is see if you live in a Universal Credit area. Follow these steps:

    1. Find your nearest Jobcentre if you dont know it.
    2. Check the list of Jobcentres that accept Universal Credit applications to find out if your nearest Jobcentre is on the list. Some Jobcentres are only accepting claims from single people - the list will say where.

    If you cant apply for Universal Credit, you should look at other benefits - use the Turn2us benefits checker to see what you can apply for. Your local Citizens Advice can also help you.

    If you live in a Universal Credit area

    To be eligible for Universal Credit, you must currently:

    • expect to earn less than 338 (after tax) in the next month
    • be between 18 years old and 60 years and 6 months old
    • be a British citizen (non-British citizens can apply in some places)
    • have a National Insurance number
    • have less than 6,000 in savings or other investments - eg shares, or property that you don't live in. This is increased to a 16,000 limit if you live in certain areas of Sutton or Croydon - see below

    If you live with your partner, you usually both need to be eligible. You'll need to:

    • live at the same address (you don't have to be married or civil partners to apply)
    • expect to earn less than 541 (after tax) combined in the next month
    • have less than 6,000 in savings or other investments between you. This is increased to a 16,000 limit if you live in certain areas of Sutton or Croydon - see below

    There are currently very limited circumstances when you can apply as a single person while being part of a couple who is living together. You can read more in the rules for how Universal Credit works for couples. 

    The following conditions will also apply (unless you live in Sutton or Croydon)

    There are fewer restrictions on applying if you live in parts of Sutton and Croydon. If you have a postcode beginning with SM5 2**, SM6 7**, SM6 8**, SM6 9** or CR0 4**,  this section does not apply to you. If you live anywhere else in the UK, you'll need to meet the conditions below as well as the conditions already listed. 

    You must have lived in the UK for the last 2 years, and not have been abroad for more than 4 weeks continuously during that time.

    You cant get Universal Credit if any of the following apply:

    • you can't work or look for work (eg because of illness or disability)
    • youre in supported or temporary accommodation
    • youre a homeowner
    • youve applied for a fitness for work note from a doctor
    • youre pregnant or have given birth within the last 15 weeks
    • you have caring responsibilities, eg for a disabled person
    • youre self-employed, or expect to earn money from self-employment in the next month
    • youre a company director or part of a limited liability partnership
    • youre in education or on a training course of any kind
    • you live in the same household as a member of the regular or reserve forces whos away on duty
    • you pay child maintenance via the Child Support Agency
    • youre an approved foster parent (even if you have no foster children)
    • youre expecting to adopt a child in the next 2 months
    • youve adopted a child within the last 12 months
    • youre homeless

    If you're not sure whether or not you can apply, contact the Universal Credit helpline or visit your local Citizens Advice.

    Universal Credit helpline
    Telephone: 0345 600 0723
    Textphone: 0345 600 0743
    Monday to Friday, 8am to 6pm

    Calls to this number can cost up to 9p a minute from a landline, or between 8p and 40p a minute from a mobile (your phone supplier can tell you how much youll pay). You can call and ask them to call you back.

    Your nearest Citizens Advice can also help.

    If you cant apply for Universal Credit, you should look at other benefits - use the Turn2us benefits checker to see what you can apply for.

    If you've applied for other benefits

    You cant get Universal Credit if you've applied for any of the following and you're waiting for a decision:

    • Jobseekers Allowance
    • Employment and Support Allowance
    • Income Support
    • Housing Benefit
    • Child Tax Credit
    • Working Tax Credit

    You cant get Universal Credit if youre appealing against a decision to not give you any of the following:

    • Jobseekers Allowance
    • Employment and Support Allowance
    • Income Support (IS)

    You cant get Universal Credit if youre waiting for a revision of a decision (including a 'mandatory reconsideration') to not give you any of the following:

    • Jobseekers Allowance
    • Employment and Support Allowance
    • Income Support
    • Housing Benefit

    You cant get Universal Credit if youre entitled to:

    • Employment and Support Allowance
    • Income Support
    • Incapacity Benefit
    • Severe Disablement Allowance
    • Disability Living Allowance
    • Personal Independence Payment

    You might be eligible for other benefits if youre not eligible for Universal Credit. Use the Turn2us benefits checker to see what you can apply for, or contact your nearest Citizens Advice.

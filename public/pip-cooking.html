Question 3: prepare and cook a simple meal

This advice is for EmailPrint

The question means preparing a simple meal for one and then heating it on a hob or in a microwave until it is safe to eat. The hob or microwave must be at waist height.

It’s helpful to answer the questions based on using this meal as an example: prepare two large potatoes for boiling, some sweet corn (from a tin) for boiling and a small piece of meat or fish to fry.

What the DWP want to know?

The DWP want to know how your condition affects your ability to prepare and cook (for example, the above meal) and not your technical ability to do so.

The questions

Try not to answer “sometimes” because either you do or you don’t use an aid or appliance. If you tick "sometimes" the DWP might think you can manage more than you can.

Explain when you do and don’t use an aid in the extra information.

You should probably tick “yes” and give more detail in the extra information if any of the following apply:

  • you sit down while preparing or cooking - a stool or similar counts as an “aid”
  • you take rest breaks while preparing or cooking
  • you sometimes use an aid
  • you use an aid
  • someone helps you
  • someone reminds you
  • someone encourages you
  • someone supervises you
  • someone stays with you to make sure you’re safe or not at risk

Extra information

Don’t ignore the extra information box - it’s a really important part of your application.

It’s your opportunity to tell the DWP how your condition affects you and give them a truer picture against which they’ll decide whether or not you can get PIP.

What to include: How you complete the task

List the items you use and explain how they help you and:

  • never miss any off because you think they’re obvious
  • never not explain how they help you because you think it’s obvious
  • always include any you don’t have but that would help you if you had them
  • If you use an aid or appliance to reduce the feeling of pain, discomfort or tiredness but still experience them it’s important you state in the extra information.

Someone prompts, reminds or encourages you

List their names and explain how and why they’re helping you.

Someone supervises you

List their names and explain why you need supervision and what the risks are to you or someone else if you don’t have it. Include the following:

  • if it would take you twice as long to complete the task as someone without your condition.
  • if you've ever had an accident or risk of an accident - give examples if you can

Someone assists you

If you'd experience any level of pain, discomfort or tiredness if they didn't help you.

You can't prepare or cook a meal

You must explain to the DWP why you can’t. Don’t exclude anything just because you think it’s obvious or the DWP won’t get a true picture of how your condition affects you.

What to include: How you the task makes you feel

There are things you can include to help the DWP get a better understanding of how your condition affects your ability to prepare and cook a meal. They'll use this information to decide whether or not you can perform the task reliably. If you can't do it reliably then it means you need more help and are more likely to get PIP.

Time it takes

If it takes you twice as long as someone without your condition state this. Try to give time estimate. If it takes you even longer on a bad day - state this.

Accidents

Tell the DWP if you’ve ever injured yourself while preparing or cooking a meal.

If this was because you didn’t receive help or supervision - make sure you say this and if this accident had on your physical and mental health.

Good days and bad days

Tell the DWP if you have good and bad days. Try to give a number for each. It’s ok to give an average, but tell them this and if it varies week to week or not.

To help you keep a record of these, use the template diary.

Pain, discomfort or tiredness

If you experience any pain, discomfort or tiredness even if it’s just sometimes or if you’ve found a way to cope

Tell the DWP how the pain, discomfort or tiredness affects you for the rest of the day / night and the consequences it has on your physical or mental health.

Write your answer on an additional piece of paper

You’ll probably have a fair bit to write in the extra information. It’s ok to write your answer on a separate sheet and attach it to your claim form. Always write the following on your additional sheet: the question number, your name and your national insurance number.

To help you, use this template additional PIP claim form sheet.
